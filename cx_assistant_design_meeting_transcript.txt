CX Assistant Product – Design Sync Meeting
Date: Jan 26
Duration: 15 minutes
Participants:
- Ananya (Lead Product Designer)
- Rahul (Senior Product Designer)
- Meera (UX Designer)
- Vikram (PM)

---

Ananya: Thanks everyone. Let’s keep this tight. Goal today is to align on goals, non-goals, and action items for the CX Assistant design.

Vikram: From a product standpoint, the primary goal is clarity. The assistant should clearly explain *why* a recommendation is shown and *what* action a CSM should take next.

Rahul: Agreed. Design-wise, the goal is to reduce cognitive load. Fewer surfaces, more progressive disclosure, and a strong default view.

Meera: I’d add trust as a goal. The AI needs to feel predictable and auditable, not magical. Designers should make data sources and confidence visible.

Ananya: Great. Let’s capture non-goals. One clear non-goal is full customization in v1. We’re not building a Figma-for-everyone scenario.

Rahul: Yes, and another non-goal is replacing human judgment. The assistant supports decisions; it doesn’t auto-execute actions.

Meera: Also non-goal: real-time chat for everything. The assistant is context-aware and proactive, not a generic chatbot.

Ananya: Perfect. Action items next. Rahul, can you explore two layout directions for the recommendation panel—inline vs side-drawer?

Rahul: Yep, I’ll have early mocks by Thursday.

Ananya: Meera, please define a lightweight trust framework—confidence indicators, data freshness, and explanation patterns.

Meera: Got it. I’ll share a draft doc and examples.

Ananya: I’ll consolidate today’s notes and align with PM on success metrics. Vikram, anything else?

Vikram: That covers it. Thanks all.

Meeting adjourned.
